Work Sample

Here are some story ideas I would execute on as a Content Analyst with access to the SeatGeek database. Each storyline could be pitched to relevant national and local news outlets for coverage, or published by SeatGeek on a blog or social media.

Click each box for details on the approach I'd take to the analysis.

Which college football fans travel most to support their team?

Which college football fans travel most to support their team?

Tickets for a college football game purchased from the geographic area of an opponent would be a good indicator that fans are making a trip into hostile territory to support their team. For example, a significant percentage of tickets to a home game in Ann Arbor, Michigan showing purchases from Columbus, Ohio would be evidence of Buckeye fans traveling north to support Ohio State. Schools that most frequently draw crowds to opposing stadiums could be labeled the most loyal fan bases.

The impact of new stadiums on ticket prices

The effect of new stadiums on ticket prices

A comparison of average team ticket prices before and after new stadiums have been built and opened - such as Levi’s Stadium (49ers, 2014), Marlins Park (Marlins, 2012), and Barclays Center (Nets, 2012) - would illustrate the impact of new stadiums on ticket costs.

Which concert venues most frequently have last-minute ticket deals?

Which concert venues most frequently have last-minute ticket deals?

A “last-minute deal” could be determined by calculating the percent difference between:
  • a) the average secondary ticket price for a given concert venue in the weeks leading up to a show and
  • b) the average ticket price for the day before and day of the show
Venues that have the largest negative percent difference (price drop). This could also be done for artists, sports teams, stadiums, and more - and broken out by day of week as well, to see if that would uncover additional insights.

The "Drake Effect" on ticket prices

The "Drake Effect" on ticket prices

A comparison of the average ticket prices for the Toronto Raptors, Blue Jays, and Maple Leafs - as well as the Kentucky Wildcats - before and after hip-hop superstar Drake formally declared his fanhood for each.

What U.S. events draw the biggest international crowds?

What U.S. events draw the biggest international crowds?

An analysis of tickets for U.S. events purchased from other countries would show which sports, teams, artists, and venues draw spectators stateside most frequently.

Projecting the price of Cubs tickets, if they had made the World Series

Projecting the price of Cubs tickets, if they had made the World Series

The rate of increase for ticket prices throughout the Cubs’ season, and through the team’s three playoff rounds, could lead to a projection for the secondary ticket market for each Cubs home game, had they gone on to face Kansas City in the World Series. NLCS tickets in Chicago were already selling for an average price of over $1000, so there’s a chance this projection would result in a record high price, which would make for a great hook and story.

Best “bang for your buck” in sports

Best “bang for your buck” in sports

The ratio of
  • a) least expensive average ticket price to
  • b) average amount of points a team scores
would illustrate where fans should go to get the most action for the cheapest price.

Preseason report: the most highly anticipated season openers

Preseason report: the most highly anticipated season openers

The hottest season openers for any sport could be calculated through an aggregation of multiple factors in the secondary ticket market: highest average ticket price, total number of transactions, rate of price increase, etc. This could be tied into a story containing information about offseason trades and signings, draft picks, and preseason rankings.

Regular season recap: how much extra did NFL fans pay to see “playoff-caliber” football?

Regular season recap: how much extra did NFL fans pay to see “playoff-caliber” football?

Once the regular season is over, an analysis of all games that took place between two eventual playoff teams - compared to the average NFL ticket price - could show the price difference for “playoff-caliber” football.

Interactive Products

I also have an interest in interactive storytelling and products. I'd love to work with the SeatGeek product and development teams to create experiences that would delight users and have a good chance of getting picked up in the press. Here's one idea:

How much would it cost to watch your fantasy baseball team play?

This web application would be based on a statistical model that calculates the secondary ticket revenue, on an individual basis, that top MLB players were responsible for throughout the 2015 season. The model would assign monetary values to each player, and then aggregate this data into a total ticket price for a user-entered fantasy team.

Please note: this is a rough draft of the idea and there are limitations to this concept.
